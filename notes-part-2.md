# DOCUMENT 1: SEBI TAKEOVER CODE (SAST Regulations, 1997)

## 1. INTRODUCTION TO TAKEOVER CODE

### Background

- Promoters form companies and offer shares to public
- Public subscribes based on promoters' credibility
- To protect investors, SEBI codified the **Takeover Code (SAST Regulations, 1997)**
- Purpose: Regulate substantial acquisition of shares and takeovers

### Main Objectives of the Takeover Code:

1. **Exit opportunity** to investors who don't wish to continue with new management
2. **Full and truthful disclosure** of all material information
3. **Sufficiency of financial resources** by promoters for acquisition payment
4. **Timely completion** of takeover formalities

---

## 2. PUBLIC OFFER (OPEN OFFER)

### Definition

Public offer is an exit opportunity given to public shareholders to dilute their shareholding in the Target Company when:

- Substantial change in shareholding pattern occurs
- Change in control of the Target Company occurs

### Key Requirement

Acquirers must **compulsorily make a public offer** to acquire **20% of shares/voting rights** from the public when the Code is triggered.

---

## 3. KEY DEFINITIONS (Important for Theory)

### Acquirer

Any person who, directly or indirectly:

- Acquires/agrees to acquire shares or voting rights in the Target Company
- Acquires/agrees to acquire control over the Target Company
- Either by himself OR with Persons Acting in Concert (PACs)

### Control

Includes:

- Right to appoint **majority of directors**
- Control management or policy decisions through:
  - Shareholding
  - Management rights
  - Shareholders agreements
  - Voting agreements
  - Any other manner

### Promoter

- Any person in **control** of the target company
- Any person **named as promoter** in offer documents or shareholding pattern
- Includes any person in the **promoter group**

### Public Shareholding

Shareholding held by persons **other than promoters**.

### Promoter Group

**(a) If promoter is a body corporate:**

- Subsidiary or holding company
- Any company where promoter holds 10% or more equity
- Companies where common 20% shareholders exist

**(b) If promoter is an individual:**

- Spouse, parent, brother, sister, child
- Companies where promoter/relatives hold 10% or more
- HUF/firm where promoter holds 10% or more

---

## 4. EVENTS TRIGGERING PUBLIC ANNOUNCEMENT (Important Table)

| Regulation     | Event Triggering Public Announcement                                                                            |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Reg 10**     | Acquisition entitling **15% or more** voting rights                                                             |
| **Reg 11(1)**  | Acquirer holding 15%-55% acquires additional shares giving **more than 5%** voting rights in one financial year |
| **Reg 11(2)**  | Acquirer holding 55%-75% acquires **any additional shares**                                                     |
| **Reg 11(2A)** | Acquirer holding 55%-75% intends to **consolidate** holdings                                                    |
| **Reg 12**     | To acquire **control** over Target Co. (whether or not shares acquired)                                         |

### Creeping Acquisition (Second Proviso to Reg 11(2))

- Acquirer with 55%-75% can acquire up to **5% additional voting rights**
- Through **open market purchase in normal segment**
- Without making a public announcement
- Total holding **must not exceed 75%**
- Can be in one or more tranches without time restriction

---

## 5. DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS

### Regulation 7 – Event-Based Disclosures

| Sub-Reg   | Event                                                 | Time Limit                             | Whom to Disclose                |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **7(1)**  | On reaching every stage of shareholding               | Within 2 days of allotment/acquisition | Target Company + Stock Exchange |
| **7(1A)** | Purchase/sale aggregating 2% or more of share capital | Within 2 days                          | Target Company + Stock Exchange |
| **7(3)**  | Shares acquired under Reg 7(1) & 7(1A)                | Within 2 days                          | Target Company + Stock Exchange |

### Regulation 8 – Yearly Disclosures

| Sub-Reg  | Who Must Disclose                   | Time Limit                               | What to Disclose                            |
| -------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **8(1)** | Person holding more than 15% shares | Within 21 days from FY end (Mar 31)      | Holdings as on 31st March                   |
| **8(2)** | Promoters/persons having control    | Within 21 days from FY end + record date | Holdings of self and PACs                   |
| **8(3)** | Listed Company                      | Within 30 days from FY end + record date | Changes in 15%+ holdings; Promoter holdings |

---

## 6. EXEMPTIONS UNDER TAKEOVER CODE (Important)

The following are exempt from public offer requirement:

1. **Allotment from public issue**
2. **Rights issue allotment** (within entitlement and Reg 11 limits)
3. **Underwriting agreements**
4. **Inter-se transfer** among:
   - Group as defined under MRTP Act, 1969
   - Relatives under Section 6 of Companies Act, 1956
   - Qualifying Indian promoters/foreign collaborators
   - Qualifying Promoters
5. **Ordinary course of business acquisitions** by:
   - Registered stock brokers
   - Market makers
   - Public Financial Institutions
   - Banks/PFIs as pledgees
6. **Transmission/inheritance**
7. **Government companies** (Section 617)
8. **Schemes under:**
   - Section 18 of Sick Industrial Companies Act, 1985
   - Arrangement/reconstruction/amalgamation
9. **Unlisted companies' shares**

# DOCUMENT 2: MERGER OF COMPANIES IN NATIONAL/PUBLIC INTEREST

## 1. LEGAL PROVISIONS

### Section 396 of Companies Act, 1956 (Section 237 of Companies Act, 2013)

**Powers of Central Government to provide for Amalgamation in National Interest**

> Note: Words "public interest" were substituted for "national interest" by Section 152 of the Companies (Amendment) Act, 1960.

---

## 2. KEY PROVISIONS

### Section 396 Highlights:

- Central Government can order amalgamation if **essential in public interest**
- Order must be notified in **Official Gazette**
- Order must be laid before **both Houses of Parliament** (Parliamentary scrutiny)
- The amalgamated company shall have specified:
  - Constitution
  - Property, powers, rights
  - Interests, authorities, privileges
  - Liabilities, duties, obligations

### Protection of Stakeholders

- Old shareholders, debenture holders, and creditors must have **same interest** in resulting company
- If they receive less, they are entitled to **compensation**
- Compensation assessed by **prescribed authority**

### Appeal Process

- Aggrieved member/creditor can file appeal within **30 days** of publication
- Appeal goes to **Company Law Board/Tribunal**
- Compensation paid by the resulting amalgamated company

---

## 3. CONDITIONS FOR PASSING ORDER

No order shall be made unless:

**(a)** Copy of proposed order sent in draft to each company concerned

**(b)** Central Government considers and modifies based on:

- Suggestions from companies
- Objections received
- Within **minimum 2 months** from receipt

**(c)** Copies of every order laid before both Houses of Parliament

---

## 4. SECTION 396A: PRESERVATION OF BOOKS AND PAPERS

### Key Provisions:

- Books and papers of amalgamated company **cannot be disposed** without prior Central Government permission
- Government may appoint a person to examine books for evidence of:
  - Offences in promotion/formation
  - Management of affairs
  - Amalgamation-related offences

---

## 5. RATIONALE

- Government amalgamates companies for **general welfare** and **well-being** of people
- Some cases require urgent action where normal procedures cause delays
- Delays would be **detrimental to national interest**

# DOCUMENT 3: ACQUISITIONS – CONCEPTS AND DIFFERENCES

## 1. MERGER VS ACQUISITION (Important Comparison)

| Basis                | Merger                                                       | Acquisition                                  |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Definition**       | Two/more companies combine to form one company with new name | One company acquires stake (>50%) of another |
| **Nature**           | Strategic decision to combine                                | Purchase of shares for control               |
| **New Identity**     | New name created                                             | Acquired company may keep or lose name       |
| **Atmosphere**       | Friendly                                                     | May be friendly or hostile                   |
| **Information Flow** | Pre-shared with directors, employees                         | May be sudden/forced                         |
| **Decision Making**  | Joint planning                                               | Imposed by acquirer                          |
| **Resource Sharing** | Mutual benefit                                               | Acquirer gains control                       |
| **Employee Impact**  | Smooth transition                                            | May create unease                            |

### Key Differences Explained:

- **Merger:** Helps share information, technology, resources – increases overall strength
- **Acquisition:** Acquirer imposes decisions on staffing, structure, resources

---

## 2. HOSTILE TAKEOVER

### Definition

When the management of the target company is **unwilling to negotiate**, the acquirer can:

- Approach **directly to shareholders**
- Make an **open offer**
- This is called a **Hostile Takeover**

## 5. REASONS FOR ACQUISITIONS (Important for Theory)

### Reason 1: Small firm cannot face competition

**Example:** Taxi for Sure sold to Ola

- Ola and TaxiForSure operated as separate entities initially
- 1,700 employees retained
- Founders moved to advisory roles

### Reason 2: To Reduce Debt Burden

**Example:** Etihad acquiring stake in Jet Airways

- Currently at 26%, capped at 49%
- Rights shares to be issued if open offer fails

### Reason 3: Personal/Financial Obligations

**Example:** Siddhartha selling stake in Mindtree

- Owned 21% directly and through Café Coffee Day
- Sold to meet personal obligations

### Reason 4: Diversification

**Example:** LIC buying stake in IDBI

- LIC entered diverse area of banking
- Used channel to sell insurance products

# DOCUMENT 4: ACQUISITION PROCESS (DETAILED)

## 1. TYPES OF ACQUISITIONS

### (A) Friendly Acquisition

- Companies cooperate in negotiations
- Both managements agree to terms

### (B) Hostile Acquisition

- Target is unwilling to be bought
- Target's board has no prior knowledge
- Direct approach to shareholders

### (C) Reverse Takeover

- Smaller firm acquires larger/longer established company
- Combined entity keeps the smaller firm's name
- **Example:** Warren Buffett buying Berkshire Hathaway (textile company), merged with his private insurance business to convert his investment vehicle into a public entity

### (D) Reverse Merger

- Private company gets publicly listed in short time
- Private company with strong prospects buys publicly listed company (usually shell company with no business)
- **Examples:**
  - ICICI and ICICI Bank
  - Purple Finance Limited (BSE listing in June 2024 via reverse merger with Canopy Finance Ltd)
  - Burger King via Justice Holdings (2012) – 3G Capital re-listed Burger King

---

## 2. ACQUISITION PROCESS – STEP BY STEP

### Step 1: Appointment of Merchant Banker

- Acquirer must appoint a **Category-I Merchant Banker** registered with SEBI
- MB advises on the acquisition
- MB makes public announcement on acquirer's behalf

### Step 2: Use of Media for Announcement

Public announcement to be made in:

- **One National English Daily**
- **One Hindi Daily**
- **One Regional Language Daily** (where shares are listed and traded)

### Step 3: Timing of Announcement

- Within **4 days** of:
  - Finalization of negotiations, OR
  - Entering into agreement/MOU, OR
  - Decision to acquire shares/voting rights

---

## 3. CONTENTS OF PUBLIC ANNOUNCEMENT (Important)

The public announcement must contain:

1. **Paid-up share capital** of target (fully + partly paid)
2. **Number and percentage** of shares to be acquired (minimum 20% as per Reg 21)
3. **Minimum offer price** per share (fully and partly paid)
4. **Mode of payment** of consideration
5. **Identity of acquirer** (and promoters if company)
6. **Existing holding** of acquirer including PACs
7. **Salient features of agreement:**
   - Date of agreement
   - Name of seller
   - Acquisition price
   - Mode of payment
   - Number/percentage of shares
8. **Highest and average price** paid by acquirer in last 12 months
9. **Objects and purpose** of acquisition + future plans
10. **Date of dispatching** Letters of Offer
11. **Date of opening/closure** of offer
12. **Date of payment** of consideration
13. **Disclosure of firm financial arrangements** (Indian/foreign sources)
14. **Provision for unregistered owners**
15. **Statutory approvals required** (Companies Act, MRTP Act, etc.)
16. **Approvals of banks/financial institutions**
17. **Minimum level of acceptance**
18. **Other essential information**

---

## 4. KEY CONCEPTS IN PUBLIC ANNOUNCEMENT

### What is a Public Announcement?

Made in newspapers by the acquirer disclosing intention to acquire shares from existing shareholders by means of an **open offer**.

### Objective

To ensure shareholders of target company are **aware of exit opportunity** available to them.

### Required Disclosures Include:

- Offer price
- Number of shares to be acquired
- Identity of acquirer
- Purpose of acquisition
- Future plans
- Change in control
- Procedure for accepting tendered shares
- Period for completing formalities

### When Required?

- Within **4 working days** of agreement/decision
- For indirect acquisition: within **3 months** of consummation

## 6. SPECIAL TYPES OF OFFERS

### (A) Competitive Offer

- Made by **another person** (not the original acquirer)
- Must be made within **15 working days** of Detailed Public Statement (DPS)
- Original acquirer can revise offer (favorable to shareholders)
- Bidders can revise price up to **3 working days** before opening
- All competing offers must follow **identical timelines**

### (B) Conditional Offer

- Acquirer stipulates **minimum level of acceptance**
- If valid tenders are less than minimum → acquirer **not bound** to accept any shares

### (C) Minimum Level of Acceptance

- Minimum number of shares acquirer wants under conditional offer

---

## 7. FREQUENTLY VS INFREQUENTLY TRADED SHARES

### Frequently Traded

- Traded turnover during 12 months preceding PA month is **at least 10%** of total shares

### Infrequently Traded

- Turnover **less than 10%** of total shares

---

## 8. CALCULATION OF OPEN OFFER PRICE (Important for Numericals)

### For Frequently Traded Shares – Highest of:

1. **Highest negotiated price** under SPA triggering the offer
2. **Volume-weighted average price** of shares acquired by acquirer in **52 weeks** preceding PA
3. **Highest price paid** for any acquisition by acquirer in **26 weeks** preceding PA
4. **Volume-weighted average market price** for **60 trading days** preceding PA

### For Infrequently Traded Shares – Highest of:

1. **Highest negotiated price** under SPA
2. **Volume-weighted average price** in 52 weeks preceding PA
3. **Highest price paid** in 26 weeks preceding PA
4. **Price determined** by acquirer and manager based on:
   - Book value
   - Comparable trading multiples
   - Other customary valuation parameters

> Note: SEBI may require independent valuation by another merchant banker or chartered accountant (10+ years experience).

## 5. PROCEDURE FOR ACCEPTANCE (Detailed)

### Stock Exchange Mechanism

- Implemented via **separate window (Acquisition Window)** on BSE
- Per SEBI Circular dated April 13, 2015 and December 9, 2016

### For Dematerialized Shares:

1. Approach Selling Broker
2. Transfer shares to early pay-in account of Indian Clearing Corporation
3. Submit Delivery Instruction Slips (DIS)
4. Receive Transaction Registration Slip (TRS)
5. Custodian confirmation (if applicable)

### For Physical Shares:

Required documents:

1. Form of Acceptance (duly completed and signed)
2. Original Share Certificate(s)
3. Valid Form SH-4 (Securities Transfer Form)
4. Self-attested PAN copy
5. Other relevant documents (POA, Board Resolution)
6. Self-attested address proof

### Settlement Process

1. Reconciliation of acceptances by Manager and Registrar
2. Final list provided to Designated Stock Exchange
3. Settlement via Clearing Corporation
4. Direct fund pay-out to shareholders
5. Excess shares returned to demat accounts

## SAMPLE NUMERICAL PROBLEMS

### Problem 1: Trigger Threshold

**Question:** Mr. X currently holds 14% shares in ABC Ltd. He acquires additional 2% shares. Is public announcement required?

**Solution:**

- Total holding = 14% + 2% = 16%
- Crosses 15% threshold under Regulation 10
- **Public Announcement is REQUIRED**

---

### Problem 2: Creeping Acquisition

**Question:** Mr. Y holds 60% shares. Can he acquire 4% more without public announcement?

**Solution:**

- 60% + 4% = 64% (below 75% limit)
- Within 5% creeping acquisition limit
- Through normal market segment
- **No public announcement required**

---

### Problem 3: Offer Price Calculation

**Question:** Calculate minimum offer price given:

- Negotiated price: ₹100
- 52-week VWAP: ₹95
- 26-week highest: ₹105
- 60-day VWAP: ₹98
- Shares are frequently traded

**Solution:**
Highest of all four = ₹105
**Minimum Offer Price = ₹105**

---

### Problem 4: Maximum Consideration

**Question:** Open offer for 20% of 1,00,00,000 shares at ₹50 per share. Calculate:
(a) Shares to be acquired
(b) Maximum Consideration
(c) Minimum Escrow Amount

**Solution:**
(a) Shares = 20% × 1,00,00,000 = **20,00,000 shares**
(b) Maximum Consideration = 20,00,000 × ₹50 = **₹10,00,00,000**
(c) Minimum Escrow = 25% × ₹10,00,00,000 = **₹2,50,00,000**
